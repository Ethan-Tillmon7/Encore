Key features
Conflict resolver
Core feature
When two artists you want to see overlap, the app surfaces the conflict, shows set times side by side, pulls your Spotify affinity score for each, and shows how many crew members are going to each — then lets you decide with one tap.
Visual timeline highlights overlapping blocks in amber
Spotify match score shown per artist (e.g. "94% match · 38 plays last 6 months")
Crew vote indicator: "3 friends going to LCD Soundsystem, 1 going to Odesza"
One-tap resolve: pick one, or mark both as "want to split"
Crew schedule overlay
Social core
Invite your group into a shared crew. See a merged schedule view showing everyone's picks — shared sets highlighted in green, splits shown in split-color blocks. The single most useful screen during the actual festival weekend.
Crew invite via link or QR code — no account required to join
Merged timeline: color-coded per person, shared sets show overlapping avatars
Live status during festival: "At What Stage · Last seen 4 min ago"
Meetup pin: drop a "meet us here after this set" marker on the map
Group chat scoped to the festival weekend only
Spotify-powered lineup ranking
Data feature
Connect Spotify once. Every artist on the Bonnaroo lineup gets ranked by your listening history — plays, recency, saves. Artists you've never heard get a "sounds like" tag based on your top genres. Surfaces hidden gems before you've done any research.
Tier labels: Must-see · Worth checking out · Explore · Unknown

Design principles
Offline-first
Bonnaroo cell service is notoriously bad. Schedules, maps, and crew data must work without a signal. Sync when connected, function when not.
Dark mode native
Festival apps are used at night in loud dark fields. High contrast, large tap targets, minimal reading required to get the key info fast.
Glanceable over detailed
You're between sets with 10 minutes to decide. Every key screen must communicate its core info in under 3 seconds.
Crew is the product
Solo users get a great scheduler. But the real value unlock is the group layer — that's the thing that makes people text the link to their whole friend group before Bonnaroo.
